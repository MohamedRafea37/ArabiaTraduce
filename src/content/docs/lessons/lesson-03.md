# Spanish vowels

Spanish vowels almost always have the same sound.

| Letter | Sound | Similar English sound |
|---------|-------|----------------------|
| A | ah | father |
| E | eh | bed |
| I | ee | machine |
| O | oh | note (shorter) |
| U | oo | food |

Examples:

| Spanish | Pronunciation |
|----------|---------------|
| Casa | KAH-sah |
| Mesa | MEH-sah |
| Libro | LEE-broh |
| Lobo | LOH-boh |
| Uno | OO-no |

Unlike English, Spanish vowels are very consistent.

# Pronunciation tips

### H is silent

Examples:

- Hola
- Hombre
- Hotel

The **H** is not pronounced.

---

### J sounds like an English H

Examples:

- José
- Jueves
- Jugo

---

### Ñ sounds like "ny"

Examples:

- Niño
- España
- Mañana

---

### LL often sounds like "y"

Examples:

- Llamo
- Calle
- Lluvia

Depending on the country, pronunciation may vary.

---

### R and RR

Spanish has two R sounds.

Single **R**

- Pero (but)

Rolled **RR**

- Perro (dog)

Rolling the R takes practice and is completely normal to learn gradually.

# Subject pronouns

Subject pronouns tell us who is performing the action.

| English | Spanish |
|----------|----------|
| I | Yo |
| You (informal) | Tú |
| He | Él |
| She | Ella |
| You (formal) | Usted |
| We | Nosotros / Nosotras |
| You all | Vosotros / Vosotras* |
| They | Ellos / Ellas |
| You all (formal) | Ustedes |

> **Note:** In most of Latin America, **Ustedes** is used instead of **Vosotros**.

---

# The verb **ser**

**Ser** is used for permanent characteristics.

Think of things that usually don't change.

Examples include:

- Name
- Nationality
- Profession
- Personality
- Physical description
- Time
- Date

---

## Conjugation of ser

| Subject | Ser |
|----------|-----|
| Yo | Soy |
| Tú | Eres |
| Él / Ella / Usted | Es |
| Nosotros | Somos |
| Vosotros | Sois |
| Ellos / Ustedes | Son |

---

## Examples

Soy estudiante.

I am a student.

---

Ella es profesora.

She is a teacher.

---

Somos amigos.

We are friends.

---

Él es español.

He is Spanish.

---

# The verb **estar**

**Estar** is used for temporary conditions or locations.

Use it for:

- Feelings
- Emotions
- Health
- Location
- Temporary situations

---

## Conjugation of estar

| Subject | Estar |
|----------|--------|
| Yo | Estoy |
| Tú | Estás |
| Él / Ella / Usted | Está |
| Nosotros | Estamos |
| Vosotros | Estáis |
| Ellos / Ustedes | Están |

---

## Examples

Estoy cansado.

I am tired.

---

Ella está feliz.

She is happy.

---

Estamos en casa.

We are at home.

---

El libro está sobre la mesa.

The book is on the table.

---

# Ser vs Estar

A simple way to remember:

| Use **Ser** for... | Use **Estar** for... |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Identity | Feelings |
| Name | Location |
| Nationality | Health |
| Profession | Temporary conditions |
| Personality | Current emotions |
| Time | Temporary states |
| Date | |

---

## Examples

Soy Mohamed.

I am Mohamed.

---

Soy ingeniero.

I am an engineer.

---

Estoy en la oficina.

I am at the office.

---

Estoy feliz.

I am happy.

# Gender agreement

Many adjectives change depending on whether they describe a male or a female.

Examples:

Male

- Cansado
- Alto
- Ocupado

Female

- Cansada
- Alta
- Ocupada

Examples:

Soy alto.

I am tall. (male)

---

Soy alta.

I am tall. (female)

---

Estoy cansado.

I am tired. (male)

---

Estoy cansada.

I am tired. (female)

# Mini dialogue

### English

**A:** Hello! How are you?

**B:** I am very well.

**A:** Are you a student?

**B:** Yes, I am.

**A:** Where are you?

**B:** I am at home.

---

### Spanish

**A:** ¡Hola! ¿Cómo estás?

**B:** Estoy muy bien.

**A:** ¿Eres estudiante?

**B:** Sí, lo soy.

**A:** ¿Dónde estás?

**B:** Estoy en casa.
