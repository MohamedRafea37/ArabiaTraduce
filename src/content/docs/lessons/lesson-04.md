# Lesson 4: Regular Verbs and the Present Tense

Welcome to Lesson 4!

Now that you know the difference between **ser** and **estar**, it's time to learn how to use regular verbs in the present tense.

The present tense is one of the most important parts of Spanish because it allows you to talk about your daily life, hobbies, work, and routines.

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to:

- Conjugate regular verbs.
- Build complete sentences.
- Describe your daily routine.
- Ask simple questions about everyday activities.

---

# Learning objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

- Recognize the three verb groups.
- Conjugate regular verbs in the present tense.
- Build simple affirmative and negative sentences.
- Ask questions using regular verbs.

# Spanish infinitives

Spanish verbs end in one of three ways:

- **-AR**
- **-ER**
- **-IR**

Examples:

| English | Spanish |
|----------|----------|
| To speak | Hablar |
| To work | Trabajar |
| To study | Estudiar |
| To eat | Comer |
| To learn | Aprender |
| To read | Leer |
| To live | Vivir |
| To write | Escribir |
| To open | Abrir |

---

# Regular -AR verbs

Example: **Hablar** (To speak)

| Subject | Conjugation |
|----------|-------------|
| Yo | Hablo |
| Tú | Hablas |
| Él / Ella / Usted | Habla |
| Nosotros | Hablamos |
| Vosotros | Habláis |
| Ellos / Ustedes | Hablan |

Examples:

- Hablo español.
- Tú hablas inglés.
- Nosotros hablamos mucho.

---

# Regular -ER verbs

Example: **Comer** (To eat)

| Subject | Conjugation |
|----------|-------------|
| Yo | Como |
| Tú | Comes |
| Él / Ella / Usted | Come |
| Nosotros | Comemos |
| Vosotros | Coméis |
| Ellos / Ustedes | Comen |

Examples:

- Como arroz.
- Ella come pollo.
- Comemos juntos.

---

# Regular -IR verbs

Example: **Vivir** (To live)

| Subject | Conjugation |
|----------|-------------|
| Yo | Vivo |
| Tú | Vives |
| Él / Ella / Usted | Vive |
| Nosotros | Vivimos |
| Vosotros | Vivís |
| Ellos / Ustedes | Viven |

Examples:

- Vivo en Bahréin.
- Ellos viven en Madrid.
- Vivimos cerca del aeropuerto.

---

# Sentence structure

Spanish sentence order is usually:

**Subject + Verb + Object**

Examples:

Yo estudio español.

I study Spanish.

---

Ella trabaja en un hotel.

She works at a hotel.

---

Nosotros comemos pizza.

We eat pizza.

---

# Making negative sentences

Simply place **no** before the verb.

Examples:

No hablo francés.

I do not speak French.

---

No trabajo hoy.

I am not working today.

---

No vivimos aquí.

We do not live here.

---

# Asking questions

Raise your voice when speaking, or use question marks in writing.

Examples:

¿Hablas español?

Do you speak Spanish?

---

¿Trabajas aquí?

Do you work here?

---

¿Comen juntos?

Do they eat together?

# Mini dialogue

### English

**A:** Do you study Spanish?

**B:** Yes, I study every day.

**A:** Do you work?

**B:** Yes, I work in an office.

---

### Spanish

**A:** ¿Estudias español?

**B:** Sí, estudio todos los días.

**A:** ¿Trabajas?

**B:** Sí, trabajo en una oficina.
