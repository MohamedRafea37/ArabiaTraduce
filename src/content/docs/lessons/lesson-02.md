# Lesson 2: Numbers, Days, Dates, and Everyday Expressions

Welcome to Lesson 2!

In this lesson, you'll expand your vocabulary by learning numbers, days of the week, months, dates, and useful everyday expressions. These are essential for introducing yourself further, making plans, shopping, and asking for information.

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to:

- Count from 0 to 100.
- Say the days of the week.
- Name the months.
- Ask and answer today's date.
- Use common everyday expressions.

---

## Learning objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

- Count in Spanish.
- Say today's date.
- Ask what day it is.
- Ask someone's age.
- Use common everyday expressions.

## Forming larger numbers

To make numbers between the tens, use **y** (and).

Examples:

- 21 — Veintiuno
- 22 — Veintidós
- 25 — Veinticinco
- 31 — Treinta y uno
- 42 — Cuarenta y dos
- 58 — Cincuenta y ocho
- 76 — Setenta y seis
- 99 — Noventa y nueve

---

# Asking age

To ask someone's age:

**¿Cuántos años tienes?**

**How old are you?**

Example:

**Tengo 25 años.**

I am 25 years old.

Notice that Spanish uses **Tengo** ("I have"), not "I am."

# Saying the date

To ask:

**¿Qué fecha es hoy?**

What is today's date?

Example:

**Hoy es el 10 de mayo.**

Today is May 10th.

Another example:

**Hoy es el 3 de septiembre.**

Today is September 3rd.

# Grammar tip

## Capital letters

Unlike English, the following are **not normally capitalized** in Spanish:

- Days of the week
- Months
- Nationalities
- Languages

Examples:

- lunes
- enero
- español
- inglés

# Mini dialogue

### English

**A:** Hello!

**B:** Hello!

**A:** What day is today?

**B:** Today is Tuesday.

**A:** What is today's date?

**B:** Today is June 15th.

---

### Spanish

**A:** ¡Hola!

**B:** ¡Hola!

**A:** ¿Qué día es hoy?

**B:** Hoy es martes.

**A:** ¿Qué fecha es hoy?

**B:** Hoy es el quince de junio.
