Learning Spanish is easier when you build a strong foundation. This guide is designed for English speakers who are starting from scratch. By the end of this lesson, you will be able to greet people, introduce yourself, and understand basic everyday expressions.

## Learning Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

- Greet someone in Spanish.
- Introduce yourself.
- Ask someone their name.
- Respond to simple questions.
- Pronounce common Spanish words.

## Basic Greetings

| English | Spanish | Pronunciation |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Hello | Hola | OH-lah |
| Good morning | Buenos días | BWEH-nos DEE-as |
| Good afternoon | Buenas tardes | BWEH-nas TAR-des |
| Good evening / Good night | Buenas noches | BWEH-nas NO-ches |
| Goodbye | Adiós | ah-DYOS |
| See you later | Hasta luego | AH-sta loo-EH-go |

## Introducing Yourself

### English

Hello, my name is John.

### Spanish

**Hola, me llamo Juan.**

Useful phrases:

| Spanish | English |
|----------|----------|
| Me llamo... | My name is... |
| Soy... | I am... |
| Mucho gusto. | Nice to meet you. |

### Example Conversation

**Person A**

Hola.

**Person B**

Hola.

**Person A**

Me llamo David.

**Person B**

Mucho gusto.

## Asking Someone's Name

### Question

**¿Cómo te llamas?**

## Grammar Tip

Spanish nouns have grammatical gender.

Generally:

- Words ending in **-o** are masculine.
- Words ending in **-a** are feminine.

Examples:

| Spanish | English |
|----------|----------|
| El libro | The book |
| La casa | The house |

> **Note:** There are exceptions, so you'll learn more about noun gender in a later lesson.
